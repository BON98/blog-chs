CPR FOR AN ADULT

• If you have not had training in CPR ,or unwilling or unable to give rescue breaths you can give chest compressions only.
•If the casualty vomits during CPR ,roll him away from you onto his side, with his head turned towards the floor to allow vomit to drain.
-Clear his mouth, then immediately roll him onto his back again restart CPR.
• If there are more than one rescuer ,change over every 1-2 minutes, with minimal interruption.
• Ask a helper to fetch an AED(automated external defibrillator)-device that helps those experiencing sudden cardiac arrest.
Action
1.Position hands on chest
-Place one hand on the centre of the casualty’s chest.
-Place the heel of your other hand on top of the first and interlock your fingers off the casualty’s ribs.
2.Give 30 chest compressions.
-lean directly over the casualty’s chest and press down verticallynabout 5-6cm(2.5inches).
-Release the pressure ,but do not remove your hands.
-Give 30 compressions at a rate of 100-120 per minute.
3.Open airway,begin rescue breaths
-Tilt the casuaty’s head with one hand and lift the chin withtwo fingers of the other hand.
-Pinch the nostrils closed, and allow his mouth to fall open.
-Take a breath, seal your lips over the caasualty’s mouth and blow steadily until the chest rises.
4.Watch chest fall
-Maintain the open airway, take your mouth away from the casualty’s.
-Look along the chest and watch it fall.
-Repeat to give TWO rescue breaths; each full breath should take one second.
-Repeat 30 chest compressions followed by TWO rescue breathes.
5.Continue CPR until emergency help arrives or casualty recovers.

CPR FOR A CHILD(ONE YEAR TO PUBERTY)

Used: 4.CHOCKING CHILD-1 year to puberty Caution
• If you have not had training in CPR ,or unwilling or unable to give rescue breaths you can give chest compressions only.
• If the casualty vomits during CPR ,roll him away from you onto his side, with his head turned towards the floor to allow vomit to drain.
-Clear his mouth, then immediately roll him onto his back again restart CPR.
• If there are more than one rescuer, change over every 1-2 minutes, with minimal interruption.
• Ask a helper to fetch an AED(automated external defibrillator),ideally with pediatric pads.
Action
1.Check that airway is open
-Tilt the child’s head with one hand and lift the chin with two fingers of the other hand to ensure the airway is open.
2.Give five initial rescue breaths
-Pinch the nose to close the nostrils.
-Allow the mouth to fall open.
-Take a breath and seal your lips over the child’s mouth.
-Blow steadily until the chest rises,then watch it fall;a rescue breath should take one second.
-Give 5 rescue breaths.
3.Give 30 chest compressions.
-Place heel of one hand on the centre of chest.
-Lean directly over child’s chest and press down to atleast one third of its depth,then relese the pressure,but do not release hand.
-Give 30 compressions at a rate of 100-120 per minute.
4.Give two rescue breaths.
-Then repeat 30 chest compresions followed by 2 rescue breathes(30:2)for one minute.
-Call for emergency help if this has not yet been done.
-Take the child to the pjhone with you if necessary.
5.Continue CPR (30:2) until emergency help arrives;the child shows signs of becoming responsive-coughing, opening her eyes, speaking and starts breathing normally or you are to exhausted to continue.

CPR FOR AN INFANT(UNDER ONE YEAR) Used: 5.Chocking Infant (Under 1 year)

Caution
• If you have not had training in CPR ,or unwilling or unable to give rescue breaths you can give chest compresions only.
• If the infant vomits during CPR ,roll him away from you onto his side, with his head turned towards the floor to allow vomit to drain.
-Clear his mouth ,then immediately roll him onto his back again restart CPR.
• If there are more than one rescuer, change over every 1-2 minutes, with minimal interruption.
• Do not use AED on an infant.
Action
• 1.Check that airway is open
• -Tilt the child’s head with one hand and lift the chin with two fingers of the other hand to ensure the airway is open.
• 2.Give five initial rescue breaths
• -Take breath and place your lips over infants mouth and nose.
• -Blow gently and steadily into the mouth and nose until the chest rises, then watch it fall.
• -Each full breath should take about one second.
• -Give 5 rescue breaths.
• 3.Give 30 chest compressions.
• -Place heel of one hand on the centre of chest.
• -Lean directly over child’s chest and press down to atleast one third of its depth, then release the pressure, but do not release hand.
• -Give 30 compressions at a rate of 100-120 per minute.
• 4.Give two rescue breaths.
• -Then repeat 30 chest compresions followed by 2 rescue breathes(30:2)for one minute.
• -Call for emergency help if this has not yet been done.
• -Take the child to the phone with you if necessary.
• 5.Continue CPR (30:2) until emergency help arrives; the child shows signs of becoming responsive-coughing, opening her eyes, speaking and starts breathing normally or you are to exhausted to continue.

.HEART ATTACK pg262

Recognition
-There could be :
• Vice-like chest pain ,spreading to one or both arms or jaws that does not ease with rest.
• Breathlessness.
• Discomfort, like indigestion in upper abdomen.
• Collapse ,with no warning.
• Sudden dizziness or faintness.
• Casualty may have sense of impending doom.
• Profuse sweating.
ACTION
1.Call for emergency help.
-Tell ambulance control that you suspect heart attack.
2.Make casualty comfortable.
-Half–sittting position.
-Support his head and shoulder and place cushions under his knees.
3.Give casualty medication.
-One full dose of asprin tablet(300mg in total),advice him/her chew it slowly.
-If he/she has tablets or asprin for angina, allow him to take it .Help if necessary.
CAUTION
• Be aware of the possibility of collapse without warning.
• Do not give the casualty aspirin if you know that he/she is allergic to it or if he /she under 16 years of age.
If the casualty becomes unresponsive ,open the airway and check breathing. Be prepaired to begin CPR(I)CPR FOR AN ADULT/ CPR(p258-259).

SHOCK

Recognition
• Rapid pulse.
• Pale,cold,clammy skin.
• Sweating.
As shock develops:
• Rapid ,shallow breathing.
• Weak pulse.
• Grey-blue askinespecially inside lips.
• Wrakness .
• Nausea and vomiting.
• Thirst
As the brain oxygen weakens:
• Restlessness and aggressive behavior.
• Gasping for air.
• Unresponsiveness.
Action
1.Help casualty to lie down.
-treat any cause of shock such as bleeding or burns(p274).*
-lay the casualty ideally on blanket.
-Raise and support his legs above the level of his heart.
2.Call for emergency help
-ideally ask a helper to do this and inform them of suspected shock.
3.Loosen tight clothing.
-To reduce constriction at the neck ,chest and waist.
4.Keep casualty warm.
-Cover them with blanket.
-Advice casualty not to move.
-Monitor and record vital signs including:breathing,pulse and levelof response-while waiting for help to arrive.
Caution
• Do not give casualty anything to eat or drink because an anaesthic may be needed.
• No not leave casualty unattended.
• Do not let casualty to move.
• Do not try wrm casualty with hot water bottle or any other form of direct heat.
• If casualty is late stages of pregnancy ,lean her towards her left side so the pregnant uterus does not restrict blood flow to the heart.
• If the casualty becomes unresponsive check breathing ,and be praraired to begin CPR(p258-261)
